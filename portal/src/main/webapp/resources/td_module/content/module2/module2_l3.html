What data would you gather?
The first step towards preparing to conduct an interview effectively is to gather all relevant data
such as:
  • Details about the candidate you are about to interview.
  • Background about the organization the the candidate is interested in.
  • Job description of the role the candidate is applying for.
  • Technical Skills required, if any.
What would you do with the data that you gather?
  • Having the above data helps you analyze the data, identify the gaps and provide
    effective feedback to the candidate.
  • The candidate in turn can prepare the missing data accordingly for the actual interview.
What to look for in a resume?
Before beginning the screening process, review the candidate’s resume thoroughly and identify
  • Significant gaps between different jobs
  • Lack of progression in roles or responsibilities
  • Non-job-specific educational qualifications
  • Reasons for leaving past jobs
  • Contradictions or ‘red flags’, if any
What would you do with the feedback on the resume?
  • Note down important points and gaps in the resume and provide that feedback to the candidate
    after the interview.
  • Inform candidates that some organizations may require additional documentation such as cover letters,
    samples of work, project reports, data supporting achievements, references etc.
  • Direct candidates towards tools and resources to prepare an effective resume.
  • Most candidates are unable to go past the ‘Resume‘ stage. Your feedback will help them prepare
    and present their resume for success for the actual interview.
Before you determine how much time to allot for each interview,
think about the time required for the entire process.
Remember to:
  • Always check the calendar on the interview application for any scheduled interviews.
  • Block sufficient, time for the interview.
    Include time required to prepare before, during and after the interview.
  • The interview is usually scheduled for 45 minutes.
Your preparation will go a long way in preparing candidates for their success.
