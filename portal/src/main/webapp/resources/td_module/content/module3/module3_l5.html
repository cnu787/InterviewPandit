Using the STAR Technique
The STAR  (Situation, Task, Action, Result) technique is a competency-based interviewing technique you can use to gather all the relevant information about a specific capability that a job requires. This is designed to make the job application process as objective as possible by removing any biases. Inform candidates that this may be one of the techniques used by organizations during the interview process among other techniques. Therefore, they should be prepared! Click each tab below to learn more.
Questions you can ask:

‘Tell me about a time when you…’. 
‘Give us an example of a situation in which you…’.

Look for candidates to:
  • Provide context and background.
  • Present a challenge or situation they experienced or a task they needed to accomplish.
Questions you can ask:

‘What was your role in this example?’
‘So what were you required to do in this situation?’

Look for candidates to:
  • Describe the problems & challenges in the situation.
  • Explain what goal did they have to achieve or working towards?
Questions you can ask:

‘What did you say or do?’
‘What steps did you take to tackle the opportunity/rectify the problem?’

Look for candidates to:
  • Explain what action did they take?
  • Describe why they took that action and what were the alternatives.
Questions you can ask:

"What was the impact of....“
“Where else did you use this experience?”

Look for candidates to:
  • State benefits, savings, rewards, recognitions, etc.
  • Quantify/specify the outcome of the action and if they met their objective.
  • Detail the learning from this experience.
